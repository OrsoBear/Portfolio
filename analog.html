A few years ago, I started shooting on film, experimenting with cheap, expired, or damaged rolls, often with unique effects. My approach primarily revolves around testing old point-and-shoot cameras, pushing their limits and embracing their imperfections. Over time, my camera has become a constant companion, I take it with me everywhere, on trips, nights out with friends, or even during my daily routine.

Shooting on film has significantly slowed down and made my photographic process more expensive, but it has also taught me to take my time, carefully consider each shot, and think more about framing. I now shoot less, but with greater awareness, learning to appreciate every single frame.
